Exploring Your Theme Options #back to top

General Settings

Inside the General theme options you will find the ability to upload logos (desktop and mobile), as well global background images, define your image resizer scripts, add favicon, menu related options and much more. These general options are usually options not found in the other category specific tabs but when in doubt, feel free to contact us for buyer support.

Home Settings

These settings are intended to be used on the 'dynamic homepage' and not a static homepage. If you assigned your homepage as a Static Page via "Settings >> Reading" then you would customize your homepage settings via the page you've assigned as your homepage. Otherwise, the dynamic homepage which requires no additional page setups is all available here. Assign your categories, transition effects, enable titles, descriptions, change order of the images and also the ability to assign videos instead of image backgrounds.

Style Options

Here you will be able to personalize and customize your installation by adding unique colours combinations for things such as Text, Links, Menu Items, Theme Opacity / Transparency, Font Sizes, Font Families (using Google Web Fonts) and much more.

Pages / Posts

Here you'll be able to manage the options related to your Pages & Posts on a global level (which can also be managed on a per-page/post basis). Gallery / Portfolio

Here you'll be able to control a few options related to Galleries and Portfolios, though most importantly, the ability to disable lightbox titles/descriptions and social share. Portfolios with Shortcodes

We recommend and suggest creating your Portfolio pages with shortcodes vs. page templates. Shortcodes give added features and possibilities extending what's available to portfolios built with Page Templates (such as filtered portfolios).

  • Step 1 - Create some Portfolio Categories via Dashboard >> Portfolio >> Categories.
  • Step 2 - Next you'll want to create some Portfolio Posts (items) to assign to those.
  • Step 3 - After creating your posts, go create a new Page to show these Portfolio posts.
  • Step 4 - Insert the Portfolio Shortcode and customize the shortcode options.
  • Step 5 - Publish your page and now based on your shortcode, it will display those.

Portfolios with Page Templates

Page Templates are a little outdated but they're still optional (and how we originally created this). They've some drawbacks vs shortcodes as they're not nearly as flexible in their use, nor as many conditional options to personalize.

  • Step 1 - Create some Portfolio Categories via Dashboard >> Portfolio >> Categories.
  • Step 2 - Next you'll want to create some Portfolio Posts (items) to assign to those.
  • Step 3 - After creating your posts, go create a new Page to show these Portfolio posts.
  • Step 4 - On this page being created, find the Page Attributes and assign the Portfolio.
  • Step 5 - In the meta options you'll find Page & Portfolio Options for this page.
  • Step 6 - Assign it the Category (required), Order and Page Layout that you want.
  • Step 7 - Publish your page and now based on your meta options, it will display those.

Portfolio Shortcode Explained:

							[portfolio cats="" filter="false" layout="" title="true" description="true" orderby="" order="ASC" per_page="8" pagination="true"]

The above is an example of a standard Portfolio Shortcode (when inserted from the Shortcode generator). You can customize the shortcode to suit your specific needs.

Option Value Description
cats 1,2,3,4 Left blank will display all. To display specific Categories enter the ID. To show multiple separate by a comma.
filter true or false New to Version 5.0.9 you can now add Category Filters. This requires multiple categories to work.
layout 2columns, 3columns, 4columns, grid Choose how many columns to display your Portfolio items in (1 column portfolio is coming soon).
title true or false You can disable Portfolio item titles from being displayed.
description true or false You can disable Portfolio item descriptions from being displayed.
orderby menu_order, rand, date Determine the order the Portfolio will use when displayed.
order ASC or DESC Determine the order of ascending or descending when displayed.
per_page numerical count Enter the number of portfolio items you want displayed (ie., 8).
pagination true or false You can enable pagination to show after the per_page numerical count.

Working with Galleries #back to top

Included with our theme is 7 different (and unique) Galleries (and now even more with Visual Composer)!

Unlike the Portfolios, you cannot link images to other pages (or websites), nor can you upload videos to these Galleries. These are strictly for the purpose of images being displayed while providing you several unique and professional gallery-types. These include Colorbox, Fancybox, Galleria, Orbit, PrettyPhoto and SlideViewer.

Galleries with Shortcodes

We recommend and suggest creating your Galleries pages with shortcodes vs. page templates. Shortcodes give added features and possibilities extending what's available to galleries built with Page Templates; plus it's ability for re-use.

  • Step 1 - Locate in your WordPress Dashboard >> Galleries >> Add New Post.
  • Step 2 - Each Gallery is essentially a post. These posts contain multiple images.
  • Step 3 - In your post locate Add Media just below the Gallery Post Title and click it.
  • Step 4 - Once opened you will have an option to Upload Files (or drag and drop).
  • Step 5 - Either by File Selection or Drag and Drop, select the images for this gallery.
  • Step 6 - After the images have uploaded click the X or click outisde the window.
  • Step 7 - Now the Add Media window should have closed and back on the Edit Post.
  • Step 8 - Save those changes and return to the Galleries overview (all posts).
  • Step 9 - On the Galleries overview locate the ID column for this Gallery post ID.
  • Step 10 - Now depending on where you want this to show, create a Page or Post.
  • Step 11 - Using the Shortcode Generator, insert the Gallery Shortcode.
  • Step 12 - Depending on your needs, use the Shortcode to customize your Gallery.
  • Step 13 - Publish the changes and now based on your shortcode, it will display those.

Galleries with Page Templates

Page Templates are a little outdated but they're still optional (and how we originally created this). They've some drawbacks vs shortcodes as they're not nearly as flexible in their use, nor as many conditional options to personalize.

  • Step 1 - Unlike with Shortcodes, Gallery Page Templates are created in single Pages.
  • Step 2 - First, let's go to Pages >> Add New and create a page for this new Gallery.
  • Step 3 - In the rightof the post locate Page Attributes, now select a Page Template.
  • Step 4 - Now choose from Colorbox, Fancybox, Galleria, PrettyPhoto or SlideViewer.
  • Step 5 - Once you've selected your Page Template you will now want to Add Media.
  • Step 6 - Locate just below the page title the Add Media button to upload your images.
  • Step 7 - Once opened you will have an option to Upload Files (or drag and drop them).
  • Step 8 - Either by File Selection or Drag and Drop, select the images for this gallery.
  • Step 9 - After selecting the images for upload, DO NOT insert them into the page.
  • It is extremely important you do not insert the images or it will cause duplicate galleries.
  • Step 10 - Now simply click the X to close this Add Media window or click outside of it.
  • Step 11 - In your meta options below the editor locate the Page / Portfolio Options.
  • Step 12 - Same as you did with the Portfolio template, you want to Select a Layout.
  • Step 13 - Publish your newly created page and those attached images will now display.

Gallery Shortcode Explained:

							[img_gallery id="" type="colorbox" layout="2" orderby="" order="DESC" description="" placement="below"]

The above is an example of a standard Gallery Shortcode (when inserted from the Shortcode generator). You can customize the shortcode to suit your specific needs.

Option Value Required Description
id 1,2,3 YES Enter the Gallery ID that you want displayed. Separate ID's by comma's when using multiple.
type colorbox, fancybox, galleria, orbit, prettyphoto, slideviewer YES This will define the type of gallery you're wanting to use in displaying these images.
layout 2, 3, 4, grid YES This defines the layout you're wanting to use when displaying your gallery images.
orderby random, custom_id, asc_order NO This defines the order in which the gallery images are displayed.
order ASC, DESC NO This defines whether they're Ascending or Descending in order.
description NO You can add a gallery description to your gallery images (this can be done in the editor too).
placement above, below NO If you choose to include a description this will be used to define where it shows (above or below).

Gallery Tips & Tricks

1. Are you seeing duplicate galleries? Chances are you inserted the attached images uploaded, do not insert your uploaded media files!
2. Are you missing thumbnails from your galleries? First, make sure your server supports GD Image. Also be sure to optimize your images.
3. When working with galleries optimizing images is important. Do not upload images larger than 1500x1500 or 1.5MB's total image size (maximum).

How to Setup a Contact Form #back to top

Two methods built in and the third using Contact Form 7

							[contact email="" message="" ][/contact]
Option Value Required Description
email you@your-email-address.com YES Enter the Gallery ID that you want displayed. Separate ID's by comma's when using multiple.

Language & Localization #back to top

Working with Poedit and Language Files

Updating your Language files requires a third-party resource called Poedit. Once downloaded and installed navigate to the kingsize >> lang >> en_EN.po file in your downloaded theme and open with Poedit. Once opened you'll be able to see all the translatable text that has already been included in English. You can modify these and once you are done you can SAVE CATALOG as a new poedit file (in the lang folder) and rename it to your local language, such as en_EN for English.

For an indepth explanation on how to translate with Poedit, we recommend you read Translating WordPress Plugins & Themes. There are also some additional steps required such as modifying the wp-config.php to assign your language files, which you can read over at WordPress, Installing WordPress In Your Language.

Localization (Language) Tips & Tricks

1. In your downloaded zip from Themeforest locate Documentation >> Extras and inside that is some Contributed Localizations from buyers ready to be used.
2. Our theme was made to be compatible with WPML.org which is a Multilingual plugin for WordPress. We do not offer support for it, but is made fully compatible.
3. If you speak or write in another language and would like to help make translations ready for localization we'd be most honoured by your help, please contact us!
